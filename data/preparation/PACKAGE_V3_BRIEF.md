# Package V3 Brief

## 1. Package Overview

- Package: `Klang Valley East Metro Line - Package V3`
- Parent programme: `Klang Valley East Metro Line`
- Employer: `Klang Valley Transit Authority`
- Main contractor: `Gamuda Infra Rail Sdn. Bhd.`
- Delivery model: `Design & Build`
- Contract value at award: `RM 1.280 billion`
- Contract duration: `36 months`
- Baseline commencement date: `01 Jul 2025`
- Baseline completion date: `30 Jun 2028`
- Package route length: `8.4 km`
- Package limits: `CH 11+200 to CH 19+600`
- Stations within package: `Pandan Gateway Station` and `Cheras North Station`

Package V3 is one of the elevated civil and interface packages under the Klang Valley East Metro Line programme. The package covers the section from the eastern side of `Ampang Central` through `Pandan Gateway Station` and `Cheras North Station`, terminating west of `Bandar Midah`.

The corridor passes through a dense urban environment characterised by constrained road reserves, live utility corridors, mixed commercial frontage, and several high-volume junctions. In addition to permanent civil delivery, the package carries significant interface obligations to support later systems access and line-wide integration.

Package V3 is considered one of the more delivery-sensitive sections of the programme because it combines high-visibility station works, constrained traffic interfaces, and corridor conditions that can materially affect later systems access. Progress within V3 has a direct influence on the readiness of downstream rail systems activities and therefore carries programme-level significance beyond its own civil footprint.

## 2. Package Scope

The package scope includes:

- `8.4 km` elevated viaduct including bored piles, pile caps, piers, pier caps, segmental launching, parapets, guideway drainage, and associated permanent civil works
- `Pandan Gateway Station`, an elevated urban station with interchange provision, concourse retail shell, and two main entrances integrated with existing roadside circulation
- `Cheras North Station`, an elevated local access station with park-and-ride connection, plant rooms, and provision for future feeder interchange enhancement
- utility diversion, protection, and reinstatement works affecting water, telecom, power, and drainage assets within the package corridor
- temporary traffic management, decking, diversions, hoarding, and access maintenance for active urban roads
- design coordination and physical interfaces for signalling, traction power, telecoms, SCADA, and M&E packages
- handover readiness for systems contractor access, equipment rooms, cable routes, and later testing and commissioning activities
- environmental mitigation, stakeholder coordination, and construction-phase public access management along the corridor

Within the package, `Pandan Gateway Station` is the principal interchange-sensitive location, while `Cheras North Station` is the principal utility- and access-constrained location. These two stations drive a substantial proportion of the package's interface risk and short-term sequencing decisions.

## 3. Package Objectives

The package has four principal objectives:

1. deliver the elevated civil structure safely and to specification
2. complete Pandan Gateway Station and Cheras North Station to the required structural, architectural, and access-readiness stage
3. achieve interface readiness for downstream rail systems installation
4. maintain delivery performance within approved time and cost tolerances while protecting safety, quality, and stakeholder obligations

Successful delivery of Package V3 is also necessary to protect the wider programme's interface dates for systems design freeze, access handover, and subsequent installation readiness.

## 4. Delivery Constraints

The package is governed by several material constraints:

- limited night working windows at selected junctions
- congested utility corridors near Cheras North Station
- phased traffic diversion requirements at Pandan Gateway
- dependency on timely interface sign-off with the line-wide systems package
- constrained laydown and crane operating zones in built-up commercial areas

The package therefore requires tighter-than-average coordination between civil, traffic, utility, and systems stakeholders when compared with less constrained sections of the line.

## 8. Baseline Risk Themes

The baseline risk profile for Package V3 includes:

- utility authority approvals and access restrictions
- unknown underground services and incomplete as-built records
- traffic management constraints in dense urban corridors
- interface lag between civil and systems design packages
- supply chain dependency for precast segments and temporary works
- stakeholder complaints affecting working windows
- changes to employer requirements after design development

Of these, utility release, traffic staging, and systems interface timing are treated as the primary control risks because slippage in any of these areas can rapidly affect both civil productivity and downstream access dates.
