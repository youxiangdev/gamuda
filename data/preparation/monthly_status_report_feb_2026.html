4. Key Developments

4.1 Weather Impact

Persistent rainfall during February reduced productive time for pier erection and constrained concrete operations at open work fronts. The team resequenced several activities, but the lost productivity was only partially recovered.

4.2 Cheras North Utility Conflict

Additional underground services were identified in the Cheras North corridor, requiring redesign of the immediate work sequence and closer coordination with utility owners. The site team expects diversion completion in March, subject to authority clearance.

4.3 Supply Chain

The precast segment supplier reported a temporary reduction in weekly output caused by mould maintenance and labour shortage. This has not yet become a critical path failure, but it is now a watch item.

5. Recovery Actions Started

  • Weekend and selected night concrete pours approved for critical viaduct fronts.
  • Alternate span erection sequence developed to reduce weather exposure.
  • Revised authority submission issued for telecom and water diversion crossing.
  • Interface war room started with civil, systems, and employer representatives.
