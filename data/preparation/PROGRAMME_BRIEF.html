Programme Brief

1. Programme Overview

  • Programme: Klang Valley East Metro Line
  • Employer: Klang Valley Transit Authority
  • Delivery model: Multi-package design and construction programme
  • Route form: Predominantly elevated urban metro corridor
  • Estimated route length: 21.6 km
  • Planned stations: 9
  • Depot: Bandar Selatan Operations and Stabling Depot
  • Baseline commencement date: 01 Jul 2025
  • Baseline targeted completion date: 30 Jun 2028

The Klang Valley East Metro Line is a new rapid transit corridor developed to improve east-west mobility across the eastern and south-eastern urban belt of the Klang Valley. The line is intended to provide direct rail connectivity between established residential catchments, emerging commercial districts, and major interchange nodes that are currently dependent on congested road corridors.

The programme is structured to deliver a new dedicated metro alignment, associated stations, depot and operations facilities, and the full rail systems required for safe and reliable passenger service.

The programme is intended to address sustained passenger growth on existing feeder corridors, recurring peak-hour congestion on parallel road routes, and the current lack of direct orbital movement between several high-demand urban catchments. In addition to transport capacity, the line is expected to support station-area redevelopment, improve first-mile and last-mile accessibility, and strengthen resilience across the wider public transport network.

2. Strategic Purpose

The East Metro Line is intended to:

  1. improve travel times between the north-eastern and south-eastern urban growth corridors
  2. reduce dependency on heavily loaded arterial roads and parallel bus corridors
  3. strengthen interchange access with existing rail and bus networks
  4. support long-term transit-oriented development at selected stations and interchange nodes

In operational terms, the line is expected to relieve demand pressure on overloaded bus corridors, improve access to established interchange stations, and provide a higher-capacity alternative to private vehicle commuting on several constrained east-west movements.

3. Corridor Description

The line extends from Taman Melawati in the north-east to Bandar Selatan Gateway in the south-east. The alignment passes through mixed residential, commercial, redevelopment, and interchange districts and is planned primarily on elevated guideway to reduce land take and interface disruption.

The planned station sequence is:

  • Taman Melawati
  • Wangsa East
  • Setiawangsa Connect
  • Ampang Central
  • Pandan Gateway
  • Cheras North
  • Bandar Midah
  • Salak Selatan Interchange
  • Bandar Selatan Gateway

Key network integration points are:

  • Setiawangsa Connect, with bus and local feeder interchange integration
  • Pandan Gateway, with provision for interchange to an existing urban rail corridor
  • Salak Selatan Interchange, with regional rail and bus transfer connectivity

These interchange locations are considered critical to the business case because they convert the East Metro Line from a point-to-point corridor into a network-strengthening connection for the wider Klang Valley rail system.

The programme also includes:

  • the Bandar Selatan Operations and Stabling Depot
  • line-wide signalling, traction power, telecoms, SCADA, and control integration
  • station access, pedestrian circulation, and local transport integration works
  • selected park-and-ride and feeder access improvements at outer-corridor stations

Station planning has been developed to balance corridor accessibility, interchange utility, and long-term development potential. Outer stations are intended to improve catchment access and feeder integration, while central interchange stations are intended to capture higher transfer volumes and support corridor-wide ridership growth.

4. Programme Scope

The overall programme scope comprises:

  • route development, final design coordination, and authority approvals
  • civil works packages covering viaducts, stations, foundations, drainage, and structural works
  • utility diversion, protection, and reinstatement along the corridor
  • depot, operations, and maintenance support facilities
  • rail systems packages including signalling, traction power, telecoms, SCADA, and operational control integration
  • testing, commissioning, trial running, and readiness for passenger operations

5. Delivery Strategy

The East Metro Line is planned as a multi-package delivery programme to improve constructability, procurement flexibility, interface control, and schedule resilience.

5.1 Rationale for Package Delivery

The programme is divided into packages for the following reasons:

  • corridor conditions vary materially between sections of the line
  • multiple civil fronts can be delivered in parallel
  • systems installation requires controlled interface management with civil completion
  • authority approvals, utility conflicts, and traffic staging differ by location
  • risk can be allocated more effectively across specialist scope packages

7. Programme-Level Risks

Principal programme risks include:

  • land, utility, and authority approval constraints across the corridor
  • incomplete underground records and unforeseen existing services
  • urban traffic management limitations in constrained road reserves
  • interface lag between civil packages and the line-wide systems package
  • supply chain constraints affecting specialist materials and equipment
  • stakeholder sensitivity in dense residential and commercial districts
  • depot and operations readiness dependencies affecting integrated testing
  • scope changes arising from late operational or stakeholder requirements

8. Governance and Reporting

8.1 Governance

  • weekly package-level delivery meetings
  • fortnightly programme interface coordination meetings
  • monthly package status reporting
  • monthly executive steering committee review

8.2 Reporting Structure

Programme reporting is issued through three levels of documentation:

  1. Programme Brief

establishes the line-wide scope, delivery strategy, milestones, and governance framework

  1. Package Brief

defines the baseline scope, commercial plan, and delivery milestones for each package

  1. Monthly Status Reports / Financial Summary / Risk Register

record time-bound delivery performance, cost movement, and risk status at package level

9. Package-Level Reporting

Monthly and commercial reporting under the East Metro Line is issued at package level. Package V3 is one of the principal elevated civil and interface packages and is reported separately in accordance with the programme governance structure.
